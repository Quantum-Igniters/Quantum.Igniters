Who are we?

We are the Quantum Igniters, a First Tech Challenge (FTC) rookie team that has also participated in First Lego League (FLL) for the last 4 seasons under our FLL team; Blazing Comets. We started our FTC team this year under the name Quantum Igniters. We are a diverse group of 9 freshmen, and one 8th grader. We are a group of young minds in the field of STEM and Robotics, where each person brings unique ideas and skills into the team. Guided by our coaches and mentors, we build, program, and compete with our robots throughout various competitions and challenges.

What is FTC?

First Tech Challenge (FTC) is a robotics program for middle and high school students (grades 7-12) run by FIRST Inspires. Students compete in teams of up to 15 to design, build, program, and operate robots to compete in a head-to-head challenge. These are some key points about FTC: STEM Skills: Students develop science, technology, engineering, and math (STEM) skills through designing, building, and programming robots. Competition: Teams compete in a game that changes every year, with the playing field and objectives presented as a new challenge. Teamwork: Collaboration is essential for FTC success. Students work together to design, build, program, fundraise, and promote their team. Mentorship: Adult coaches and mentors guide students throughout the program. Our Journey

We started our robotics journey as a rookie FLL team in the year 2019 and continued to compete in 4 seasons. Throughout this journey, we gained lots of new knowledge, skills, and experiences. Over the seasons, we won various awards and recognition. In out final season of participating, we won the State Championship and qualified for the WORLD Championship in Houston. With the experience we gained in FLL (FIRST LEGO League) and the enthusiasm we have towards STEM and robotics; we got motivated to continue this journey onto the next level of FTC.
